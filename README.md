<h3 align="left">About me:</h3>

<p>
  My relation with computers began in my 4th or 5th class. Life started revolving around computer games, installing essential software, and drivers for those games. Although I didn't fully understand everything at that time, I knew I loved computers.

However, as a student's life began, I found myself studying day and night, competing to get better marks than others. This focus on comparison and competition caused me to drift away from computers, and I ended up choosing biology in metric, pursuing a pre-medical path. I was on the verge of falling into the pit of choosing a medical path again, but then Covid-19 came as a blessing in disguise.

During my preparation days for MDCAT (Medical Universities' Entry Test), I watched a random Python programming tutorial on YouTube that appeared in my suggestion box. Intrigued, I thought let's follow along. The moment I wrote "2 + 2" on the computer, receiving the result "4," was beautiful. I clearly remember creating my first calculator program in C and proudly showing it to everyone. Long story short, I ended up joining COMSATS University for Software Engineering major.

I experimented with different languages and stacks, but when I delved into JavaScript, there was no turning back. It ignited a passion for web development in me, and that's why I joined Knowledge Streams in June 2023, enrolling in their Cohort-1 MERN program. While I could have learned web development on my own from online resources, I realized that soft skills are just as crucial as technical skills. Therefore, I chose to join an institute that focuses on both technical and soft skills. Moreover, participating in a bootcamp and working on projects made my learning journey more consistent and practical.

</p>
